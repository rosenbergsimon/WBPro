WBPro - Digital W/B System for FlightLogger

Overview

WBPro is a program designed to save time and paper in completing weight-and-balance, and other forms before flights. For the weight-and-balance process, it allows you to enter the aircraft loading information, verify the aircraft loading is correct, and upload the completed form to either a lesson attachment, or booking attachment, but at the click of only a few buttons. This program is designed to be used for students who already have strong knowledge of aircraft weight and balance in order to save some time; therefore new students should learn do fill out the paper forms before using this program. The program allows for additional implementation of other forms used by a flight school.

Note: The PIC is solely responsible for ensuring their aircraft is loaded in an airworthy manner.

How to Use

1. Navigate the menus to Weight and Balance, and click on the specific aircraft model you will be flying.
2. Select your aircraft ident from the drop-down menu, and fill in the loading information below.
3. Click on the Calculate button, and review the W/B form and fuel endurance upon landing to the right.
4. If the loading is within limits, click on the Upload button, and select your name below.
5. Once you have selected your name, enter a name for the document. (This appears on FlightLogger). If doing multiple legs on a flight, choose a suitable name, such as the routing for an xc flight.
6. Once clicking OK, the bar below should turn yellow as the weight and balance form is uploaded to FlightLogger.
7. If the upload is successful, a pop-up should appear indicating so. If you go into the FlightLogger lesson corresponding with your lesson, you will see the completed form there.
8. If entering multiple legs for a cross-country, you may type in the new loading information, re-click calculate and upload, and repeat without restarting the program.

The other forms follow a similar, user-friendly procedure.

Common Issues

This program relies on a slow process to upload documents to FlightLogger. It may take some time to upload them.

The program should give pop-up messages for most errors, either user-caused or issues with FlightLogger connections.

Ensure you have selected the correct aircraft ident. If not, issues will result with your name showing up to upload.

Ensure your instructor has attached a lesson to the booking, if not, uploading will not be possible.

If an error occurs during the FlightLogger uploading process, follow the prompt, and try again if that is suggested.

Please report any other bugs through a FlightLogger message, or through dispatch.
